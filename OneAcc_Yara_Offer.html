2. Opportunity & Evaluation Process

One Acc is building intelligent financial infrastructure. Because our products operate on accounting data and financial workflows, understanding how accounting systems behave is an important part of building great software.

We don't expect you to already be an accounting expert. What we're interested in is how you approach a new domain, how quickly you learn, and whether working on accounting software feels engaging and interesting to you.

Before moving forward with engagement, we'd like to spend a short session exploring your observations and thoughts after using a real accounting platform.

Transparency Around Compensation

Although there are still a few steps remaining in the process, we wanted to share the commercial structure now so you have complete visibility into the opportunity before investing additional time in the Xero exploration exercise.

We believe transparency is important, and we'd rather give you a clear understanding of the role, expectations, commitment, and compensation upfront rather than discuss financial details only at the end of the process.

The remaining discussions are intended to help both sides determine whether there is a strong mutual fit, particularly given the accounting-focused nature of the products we are building.

3. Xero Exploration Exercise

Recommended Preparation

Before starting, we recommend watching:

Accounting Basics Explained Simply

Step 1 — Setup Xero

  • Create a Xero trial account.
  • Open the Demo Company environment.
  • Explore the navigation and accounting workflows.

Step 2 — Create Transactions

  • Create a customer invoice.
  • Add 2–3 line items.
  • Apply tax.
  • Approve it and mark it paid.
  • Create a supplier bill.
  • Assign an expense category.
  • Approve it but leave it unpaid.

Step 3 — Review The Reports

  • Profit & Loss Statement
  • Balance Sheet
  • General Ledger

Observe how the transactions affect each report and how information flows through the system.

The Most Important Part

We'd love your honest feedback afterwards.

  • Did the workflows feel intuitive?
  • Which concepts felt easy or difficult?
  • Did the platform make sense to you?
  • Could you see yourself building software in this space?
  • Do you feel confident learning accounting concepts when required?

This exercise is as much for you as it is for us. We want to understand whether this domain feels like a good fit for your interests and strengths.
